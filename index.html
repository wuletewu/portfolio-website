About Me

I am Wuletawu Iyasu Faracho, a dedicated Computer Science lecturer at Wolaita Sodo University with over three years of experience teaching and mentoring students. I hold a Master of Science in Computer Science from Hawassa University, where I graduated with distinction and conducted research on applying machine learning models for foodborne disease classification.

My expertise spans a range of areas including software development, machine learning, data structures, algorithms, and database systems. As a full-stack developer and aspiring engineer, I am passionate about bridging the gap between theory and practical application, whether by guiding student projects, developing intelligent solutions, or exploring innovative technologies.

What drives me is the opportunity to empower learners, solve real-world problems, and contribute to the advancement of computer science. I especially enjoy working on projects that integrate machine learning, software engineering, and education to create meaningful impact.
